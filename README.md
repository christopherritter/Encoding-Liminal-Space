## HOW TO USE THIS MANUAL

**If entirely new:** Begin with Chapter 1. Execute every protocol. Work through Chapters 2-5 sequentially. Complete every experiment (EXP-nA, EXP-nB). Document everything using SDP checkpoints. Proceed to Section II only after completing Section I. Build your territory deliberately. Maintain your practitioner log throughout.

**If ML experienced:** Skim Chapters 1-3 for liminal framing. Focus Chapters 4-7: territory definition and data preparation. Move quickly through Chapter 10: environment setup. Chapters 11-12: parameter tuning and training require careful attention—application to territorial learning differs from standard ML. Chapters 13+: prompting, advanced work, recovery—read thoroughly even if technically familiar.

**If returning to iterate:** Go directly to relevant section. Bring your previous practitioner log for reference. Use earlier documentation to inform new exploration. Update your Integration Journal weekly.

**If interested in speculative work:** Complete Sections I-V first (Chapters 1-13). Only then attempt Chapters 14-15 (Dream Interfaces, Temporal/Entity Work). Maintain scrupulous documentation. Approach with experimental mindset, not expectation of results.

**If contributing or sharing:** Review Appendix H: Editorial Style Guide. Review Appendix F: Community Sharing standards. Write your contribution in dual-register format. Test thoroughly in your own practice before proposing. Submit with full documentation of testing outcomes.

**If encountering problems:** Check Appendix G: Troubleshooting as Data Interpretation. Document the problem in SDP checkpoint format. Treat error as boundary information: what is it teaching you? Review Chapter 17: Recovery protocols if system coherence has degraded.
